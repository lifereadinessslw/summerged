How to Write a Cover Letter

1A cover letter that accompanies your résumé is of great importance — it gives the employer a general impression of you before they read your work history. If the cover letter does not interest the potential employer, they may choose not to read the résumé at all. So make your cover letter effective by keeping it short and focusing on points that will make the employer want to interview you.

2Begin the letter by introducing yourself and stating the specific position you are applying for. In the middle section, explain why you are qualified for the job. Focus on two or three skills or experiences that are most relevant to the position — do not try to include everything. Connect your strengths directly to what the employer is looking for.

3In the final paragraph, thank the employer for their time, restate your interest in the position, and provide your contact information. Close with “Sincerely yours” or “Yours truly.”

4Throughout the letter, use a professional tone. Avoid informal language or slang. Check for spelling and grammar errors before sending — a cover letter with mistakes can end your chances before an interview even begins.

5A strong cover letter does not just repeat your résumé; it tells the employer who you are and why you are the right person for this job.

Question 1
According to this workplace document, what is the purpose of the MIDDLE SECTION of a cover letter?
  • ATo list every job and skill from your résumé.
  • BTo thank the employer and provide your contact information.
  • CTo explain why you are qualified, focusing on two or three relevant skills.
  • DTo introduce yourself and name the position you are applying for.
Correct: C — Paragraph 2 explicitly describes the middle section: explain qualifications, focus on relevant skills, connect to what the employer wants.
Question 2
The author says a cover letter with mistakes ‘can end your chances before an interview even begins.’ What does the author imply?
  • AEmployers are required to reject applications with errors.
  • BFirst impressions matter — errors signal a lack of care or professionalism.
  • CA cover letter is more important than the job interview itself.
  • DMost employers only read cover letters, not résumés.
Correct: B — The implication: errors create a negative impression before the employer meets you.
Question 3
What is the author’s purpose in writing this passage?
  • ATo argue that cover letters should be abolished.
  • BTo inform job seekers how to write an effective cover letter.
  • CTo persuade employers to hire candidates based on cover letters alone.
  • DTo explain why résumés are less important than cover letters.
Correct: B — The passage gives step-by-step instructions — informational/explanatory writing, not persuasive.
Question 4
According to the passage, what should a strong cover letter do that a résumé does NOT do?
  • AList all of the applicant’s previous jobs.
  • BProvide the applicant’s contact information.
  • CTell the employer who the applicant is and why they are the right person for the job.
  • DDescribe the applicant’s education and training.
Correct: C — Paragraph 5 states this distinction explicitly: the cover letter tells the employer who you are — the résumé lists facts.
★ GED TEST TIP: When reading workplace documents, ask: what am I looking for? Then go directly to the section most likely to have it — strategic readers don’t re-read the whole document for every question.

Clean Water and Community Health

1Access to clean water is one of the most pressing public health challenges facing communities today. Because contaminated water can spread diseases like cholera and typhoid, water quality has a direct effect on community health outcomes. As a result, many local governments have invested heavily in water treatment infrastructure.

2However, rural and low-income communities often face a different reality. Unlike cities with large tax bases, rural areas may lack the funds to maintain aging pipes and treatment systems. On the other hand, some rural communities have found creative solutions — including regional water cooperatives where multiple towns share the cost of a modern treatment facility.

3The problem of water access is not limited to infrastructure. In many communities, even where water is technically available, residents cannot afford their monthly water bills. One solution has been the creation of low-income water assistance programs. Therefore, addressing water access requires both physical infrastructure and financial support systems.

Signal words appear in bold. Identify what type of relationship each one signals.

Questions
Question 1
What text structure does paragraph 1 PRIMARILY use?
  • ACompare and contrast — two things placed side by side.
  • BCause and effect — one thing leads to another.
  • CProblem and solution — a problem is named and a fix is proposed.
  • DChronological — events are listed in time order.
Correct: B — ‘Because’ and ‘as a result’ are cause-and-effect signal words. The paragraph explains WHY water quality matters and WHAT HAPPENED as a result.
Question 2
What does the signal word ‘However’ at the start of paragraph 2 tell the reader?
  • AThe author is providing an example to support paragraph 1.
  • BThe author is about to contrast or qualify what was just said.
  • CThe author is continuing the same argument.
  • DThe author is summarizing what has already been stated.
Correct: B — ‘However’ always signals a contrast or qualification. The idea that follows is in tension with what came before.
Question 3
What text structure does paragraph 3 primarily use?
  • AChronological — events in time order.
  • BCompare and contrast — two options side by side.
  • CProblem and solution — a problem is identified and a fix is offered.
  • DCause and effect — one event leads to a consequence.
Correct: C — ‘Problem’ and ‘solution’ appear explicitly. The paragraph names a problem (affordability) and proposes a fix (assistance programs).
Question 4
How does paragraph 2 relate to paragraph 1?
  • AIt provides an example supporting paragraph 1’s main claim.
  • BIt introduces a contrasting situation that complicates paragraph 1’s picture.
  • CIt summarizes what paragraph 1 already stated.
  • DIt provides the cause of the problem described in paragraph 1.
Correct: B — ‘However’ signals that paragraph 2 is complicating the picture — not all communities benefit from the infrastructure investment described in paragraph 1.
★ GED TEST TIP: Signal words are the GED’s road signs. ‘Because’/‘as a result’ = cause/effect. ‘However’/‘on the other hand’ = contrast. ‘Problem/solution’ = problem-solution. Identify the signal word first, then name the structure.

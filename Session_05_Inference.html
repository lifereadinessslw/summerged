What Is a Community Health Center?

1A community health center (CHC) is a nonprofit, community-owned clinic that provides affordable medical care to people regardless of their ability to pay. Community health centers operate in medically underserved areas — neighborhoods, rural communities, and tribal lands where private doctors and hospitals are scarce or too expensive for most residents. They are funded in part by the federal government under Section 330 of the Public Health Service Act.

2Community health centers offer a wide range of services: primary care, dental care, mental health counseling, substance use treatment, pharmacy services, and more. Fees are based on a sliding scale, meaning patients pay according to their income. A family earning very little may pay nothing at all.

3In 2022, more than 1,400 community health centers across the United States served nearly 30 million patients. They are often the first — and only — health care provider for the communities they serve.

Questions
Question 1
What can you infer about why community health centers are located in ‘medically underserved areas’?
  • AThese areas have too many health clinics and need to reduce overcrowding.
  • BPeople in these areas would have no access to health care without a CHC.
  • CThe federal government requires all clinics to be built in rural locations.
  • DCommunity health centers prefer to avoid competition with private hospitals.
Correct: B — Paragraph 1 states underserved areas have scarce or too-expensive private doctors. The inference: without a CHC, residents would have no access.
Question 2
A patient earns $80,000 per year. What can you infer about what they would pay at a community health center?
  • ANothing — all services are free for everyone.
  • BThe full market rate — more than at a private clinic.
  • CA higher fee than a low-income patient, since fees are based on income.
  • DThe same fee as every other patient — fees are fixed.
Correct: C — The sliding scale means patients pay according to income. A higher-income patient would pay more — directly implied by the word ‘sliding.’
Question 3
Which statement is an inference rather than a fact stated in the passage?
  • ACommunity health centers served nearly 30 million patients in 2022.
  • BFees at community health centers are based on a sliding scale.
  • CWithout community health centers, millions of Americans would have no access to medical care.
  • DCommunity health centers receive federal funding under Section 330.
Correct: C — A, B, and D are stated directly. C requires reasoning beyond the text — it is an inference supported by the passage but not explicitly stated.
Question 4
What is the implied main idea of this passage? Write it in one sentence.
Model answer: Community health centers provide essential, affordable health care to Americans who need it most and would otherwise go without it.
★ GED TEST TIP: An inference is not a guess — it is a conclusion supported by text evidence + what you already know. For every inference, find the sentence in the passage that supports it.
